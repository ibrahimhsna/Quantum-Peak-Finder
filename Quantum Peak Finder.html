Bond Dimension (χ) Convergence

A higher bond dimension (χ) yields a more accurate MPS approximation. If χ is insufficient, the simulated probability distribution collapses, and the algorithm identifies the completely wrong bitstring.

Critical Convergence Shifts

Interpreting the Gap (Δ)

When χ=A produces a logP and χ=B (where B > A) produces logP + Δ, it means the answer from χ=A is mathematically eΔ times less probable in the more accurate state. A large gap (e.g., +91.7 nats) implies the low-χ answer is catastrophically incorrect, not just a slight deviation.
